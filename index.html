Relativity

Understanding Einstein's theory.

Born in on 14 March 1879 in Germany, Albert Einstein was a pioneering theoretical physicist who revolutionized humankind's understanding of the universe.

Einstein recognized that Newtonian mechanics, though it was conventionally accepted, cannot be reconciles with the laws of the electromagnetic field. Out of this understanding, Einstein's Theory of Special Relativity was born.

Observation is relative.

We all know that different people perceive a situation differently. This concept can be applied to physics. Depending on an observer's location relative to a phenomenon, the phenonenom itself may change.

Relativity is built on two core postulates.

1: Equivalence of Physical Laws

The laws of physics are the same in all internal frames of reference.

2: Constancy of the Speed of Light

The speed of light in a vacuum, c = 3.00 x 108 m/s, is the same in all internal frames of reference, regardless of the motion of the source or the observer of the light.
