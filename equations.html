Equations

Speed of Light in a vacuum

c ≅ 2.99792458 x 108 m / s

The speed of light is the fastest speed in the universe, and thus it is a constant in Einstein's equations. As velocity approaches the speed of light, time, distance, and mass are affected.

Time Dilation

Δt = Δt0 / √ 1 - (v2 / c2)

Dilated time interval = proper time / √ 1 - ((speed of clock)2 / (speed of light2))

Length Contraction

L = L0 √ 1 - (v2 / c2)

Contracted length = poper length x √ 1 - ((speed of object)2 / (speed of light)2

Rest Energy

E = mc2

Rest energy = (mass of object) x (speed of light)2

Relativistic Energy

E = (m0c2) / √ 1 - (v2 / c2)

Relativistic energy = (rest mass) x (speed of light)2 / √ 1 - ((speed of object)2 / (speed of light)2)
